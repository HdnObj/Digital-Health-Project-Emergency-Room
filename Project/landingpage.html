About Us

Built for the
Modern Emergency Room

City General Hospital's ERMS was designed to solve the core challenge of emergency care: information fragmentation. Our system unifies patient registration, real-time triage, bed management, clinical documentation, and discharge in a single connected platform — inspired by HL7 FHIR standards and decades of clinical workflow research.

  • Real-time triage with ESI priority scoring (P1–P3)
  • Role-based access: Admin, Nurse, Doctor, Patient portals
  • NDC drug prescriptions & ICD-10 coded diagnoses
  • Live bed capacity monitoring & automated assignment
  • Complete audit trail for every clinical action
  • Responsive design — works on any device, any screen size

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about City General's Emergency Room Management System.

How do I access the patient portal? +
Your patient portal account is created by the hospital administration after your ER visit is registered. You will receive your username and a temporary password. Log in at the ERMS login page to view your vitals, diagnosis, prescriptions, and visit timeline.
What information is visible in my patient portal? +
Once logged in, you can see: your current visit status, triage vitals (BP, HR, SpO₂, Temperature, Pain Scale), your assigned nurse and doctor, diagnosis with ICD-10 code, treatment plan, all prescriptions with NDC codes and dosages, your bed/ward location, and a full chronological visit timeline from arrival to discharge.
How does the triage priority system work? +
Our ER uses the Emergency Severity Index (ESI) with three levels: P1 Critical (red) for immediate life threats, P2 Urgent (yellow) for high-risk conditions, and P3 Non-Urgent (green) for less severe cases. The system automatically flags P1 when vital signs indicate danger — for example, if heart rate exceeds 120 bpm or SpO₂ drops below 90%.
Is my medical data secure and private? +
Yes. The ERMS is designed with HIPAA compliance in mind. Patient accounts can only view their own records. Staff accounts have role-based access — nurses see triage workflows, doctors see clinical data, and admins see operational data. Every action is logged in an immutable audit trail.
How do doctors enter prescriptions in the system? +
Physicians use the Clinical View panel to search the drug library by name or NDC code with live autocomplete. They enter dosage instructions and add drugs to the prescription list. The system checks for known drug interactions before submission and alerts the doctor if a conflict is detected.
Can the system handle multiple roles simultaneously? +
Absolutely. The ERMS supports concurrent sessions across all four roles: Admin, Nurse, Doctor, and Patient. Changes made by one role — such as a nurse recording vitals — are immediately reflected on the doctor's and admin's dashboards, creating a truly real-time clinical environment.
What happens when a patient is discharged? +
When a doctor marks a patient as "Discharged," the system automatically frees the assigned bed, updates the bed map, and logs the discharge event in the audit trail. The patient's portal remains accessible so they can review their complete visit record, diagnosis, and prescriptions at any time.
